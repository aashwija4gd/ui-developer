# Content Writing

## Voice and tone

- Keep voice consistent.
- Let tone change by context.
- Be warm in onboarding, calm in errors, direct in destructive flows.

## Writing rules

- Be specific.
- Use active voice.
- Keep copy concise.
- Use sentence case.
- Avoid jargon and internal system names.

## Placeholder text

- Placeholders are examples, not labels.
- Keep them short and visibly lighter than input text.

## Button labels

- Use verbs and specific actions.
- Avoid generic labels like OK, Yes, Submit, or Click here.

## Error messages

- Explain what went wrong and what to do next.
- Avoid technical codes as the primary message.
- Do not blame the user.

## Grammar and punctuation

- Use correct apostrophes and subject-verb agreement.
- Use one space after punctuation in normal prose.
- Use em dash without spaces, en dash with spaces for ranges, and the ellipsis character for truncation or loading.
- Never use double spaces after periods.
